5 reasons why Meri Awaaz will fail

1. Goal

“Audacity, more audacity, always audacity.” ~ Georges Jacques Danton

We are going to change how governments work around the world and make them accessible through mobile access. We are aiming big, insanely big.

2. Revenue

“The greater the obstacle, the more glory in overcoming it.” ~ Molière

While our model is clear and focussed, it does have a lot of variables. Our app and the related resources will always be open source and community driven. We do not plan to make revenue from the app ever. It will be free for our people to use. Our revenue source is through partnerships with governments.

3. Impact

“In a gentle way, you can shake the world.” ~ Mahatma Gandhi

We will be hated by the people who are comfortable with the status quo. We are okay with doing that. Change is a bit unsettling but it is inevitable.

4. Challenge

“Life is either a daring adventure or nothing at all.” ~ Hellen Keller

In our journey to change governments forever, there are numerous challenges. The journey seems impossible and the road is less travelled but within each challenge lies an opportunity; an opportunity to usher in unprecedented change.

5. Growth

“Entrepreneurs are not content just to give a fish or teach how to fish. They will not rest until they have revolutionized the fishing industry” ~ Bill Drayton

We want to go big. We want to do it now. The world needs change and we want to deliver it. We believe the right way to deliver sustainable change is through entrepreneurship and not charity.

The 1 reason we will win

"Impossible is just a big word thrown around by small men who find it easier to live in the world they've been given than to explore the power they have to change it. Impossible is not a fact. It's an opinion. Impossible is not a declaration. It's a dare. Impossible is potential. Impossible is temporary. Impossible is nothing." ~ Muhammad Ali

We have the will and the grit to make it happen. And that’s why we will win. It is time to change things because there is no such thing as small change.

“The question isn't who is going to let me; it's who is going to stop me” ~ Ayn Rand
